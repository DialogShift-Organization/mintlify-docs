The **Statistics → Chatbot AI** page shows you how your chatbot is being used. This page explains what each number means.

<Info>
Use the **date range** in the top right (default: last month). All metrics always refer to the selected period.
</Info>

## Basic metrics

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active users** | Number of people who interacted with the chatbot during the period. |
| **Live chats** | Number of chats that were handed over to your team (live chat). |
| **Auto answers** | Number of answers the chatbot gave on its own. |
| **Booking redirects** | How often the chatbot forwarded guests via a link into the booking flow on your website. |
| **Nights requested** | Number of nights from these forwarded booking requests. |

<Note>
**Booking redirects** are teased bookings: the chatbot opens the web booking engine on your hotel website via a prepared link (like a quick booker, with a pre-selected room). These are **booking requests, not completed bookings** – the actual bookings are shown further down.
</Note>

### The rates (in %)

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Auto answer rate** | How much the chatbot handled on its own, relative to chats handed over to the team. A high value means the bot answers most things itself. |
| **Live chat rate** | Share of active users who entered the live chat. |
| **IBE redirect rate** | Share of active users who triggered a booking request. |

## Commercial evaluations

<Info>
This section only appears if **booking revenue is tracked** for your hotel – either via the DialogShift tracking pixel in your booking engine or via an integration (e.g. HNS).
</Info>

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Bookings count** | Actual completed bookings from guests who used the chat. |
| **Booking EUR** | Revenue from these bookings. |
| **Booked nights** | Number of nights actually booked. |
| **EUR per night** | Average revenue per night. |

<Note>
A booking is attributed to the chat when the guest **used the chat to inform themselves and then booked** – regardless of whether they used a link from the chat.
</Note>

## Usage over time

| Chart | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active users per calendar year** | Weekly user counts compared: current year vs. previous year. |
| **Users per weekday** | Which weekdays the chat is used most. |
| **Users per hour of day** | Which times of day the chat is used. |
| **Behaviour scoring** | Distribution of users by topic/intention (e.g. hotel info, booking, food & beverages). |
| **Channel split** | Which channels users chat through (e.g. website, smartphone, WhatsApp, QR code). |
| **Reaction times live chat** | How quickly your team responded to incoming live chats (e.g. under 10 minutes). |
| **Touch points** | Which entry point opened the chats (e.g. chat button or a specific teaser). |

<Note>
Reaction times only count live chats **within your business hours**.
</Note>

## Example

How to read the numbers together:

- **16 active users**, of which **1 live chat** → live chat rate = **6%**.
- **65 auto answers** with only 1 handover → auto answer rate = **98%**: the bot handled almost everything itself.
- **4 booking redirects** for 16 users → IBE redirect rate = **25%**: one in four users triggered a booking request.
- **8 nights** from 4 redirects → on average 2 nights per request.
